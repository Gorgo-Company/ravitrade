What the app is,

This app is best understood as a decision interface for active options traders. It is not selling raw market data by itself. It is selling a faster, clearer trading workflow: structure, flow, timing, and execution in one place.

The practical business question is simple: what experience is included in the subscription, what does it cost to deliver each month, and what contribution profit remains per customer?

Best way to frame the product

This is a premium trading workflow subscription. The subscription is justified by time saved, setup clarity, and improved execution discipline — not by promising trading outcomes.

Terminal core, exposure ladder, live flow grid, execution view, timeline view, and signal lock. Those are the pieces that make the product feel complete and worth paying for every month.

1. Complete product offer for the paid subscription

Before pricing, the offer has to be clear. This section defines what the subscriber is actually getting.

Core terminal

Live price charting, symbol and interval controls, fast load times, and a clean terminal shell that lets the trader stay inside one interface instead of bouncing across multiple tools.

Structure layer

Exposure ladder with king node, support/resistance hierarchy, gatekeepers, springs, and clearly marked levels anchored to price so the user can scan the screen instantly.

Intent layer

Real-time options flow grid with filters, NBBO position, flow score, opening-vs-closing estimate, contract drilldown, and unusual activity visibility.

Timing layer

Timeline view showing flow cluster starts, level defenses, break confirmations, continuation, and exhaustion so the user can identify when a setup actually becomes actionable.

Execution layer

Execution view plus signal lock: entry zone, invalidation, target path, confidence score, and trade plan framing. This is the part that makes the system feel useful every day.

Ongoing product value

Fast visual scan, repeat use during the session, watchlist coverage, and a workflow that makes the user feel more organized and decisive than they feel using general-purpose charting alone.

Practical interpretation: the paid product should be positioned as a premium, all-in-one visual options workflow for active traders who already understand markets and want faster recognition of levels, flow, and timing.

Why $149 is the strongest core price

  • It sits in the premium retail-trader software zone without looking extreme.
  • It leaves room for direct market-data and infrastructure cost while still producing attractive monthly contribution profit.
  • It matches the visual, decision-first product you are building better than a low-cost commodity price point.

Launch recommendation

  • Launch with one main paid plan at $149/month.
  • Optionally use a smaller “beta” or “founding user” price only as a temporary conversion tool, not as the long-term anchor.
  • Add the higher tier only after the replay/journal/performance loop is strong enough to justify it.

3. Estimated monthly direct cost per active user

This section focuses on delivery cost, not total company overhead. These are the unit-level costs that matter most when evaluating what each paid account contributes.

Cost category Estimated range per active user / month Comment
Live options market-data allocation $12–$35 Depends heavily on feed arrangement, entitlements, and whether data cost is passed through or pooled in a vendor contract.
Underlying price / quote support $2–$8 Often bundled or relatively small compared with options data, but still should be accounted for.
App hosting + compute + WebSocket load $6–$14 Higher at small scale, lower as the subscriber base grows and infrastructure is utilized more efficiently.
Database / storage / caching $3–$7 Includes ClickHouse/Postgres/Redis style storage and replay-related growth pressure where applicable.
Payment processing ~3% + small fixed fee At $149 monthly, a practical estimate is roughly $4.80–$5.50 per successful charge.
Support / incident load allocation $1–$6 Small if product is stable and self-serve; higher if users require manual assistance.
Total direct delivery cost $28–$66 Reasonable working range for planning before exact vendor contracts are finalized.
Working rule: use the midpoint near $45–$50 for planning until exact data-vendor pricing, hosting architecture, and support pattern are known.

6. Market outlook from an owner’s perspective

This section is about business opportunity, not valuation language. The question is whether the addressable category is large enough and active enough to support meaningful recurring revenue.

Category logic

  • The broad online trading platform market is already a multi-billion-dollar category and continues to grow.
  • The AI trading platform market is also already large and growing quickly, which supports demand for a premium interface built around automation, interpretation, and speed.
  • Your app sits inside a more focused niche: active options traders who already pay for premium information and workflow tools.

Practical market framing

  • You do not need a massive market share to build a strong business.
  • This category supports a subscription business if the app becomes part of the trader’s daily routine.
  • The opportunity is best measured in retained paying users, not broad casual traffic.

Reachable niche

Active retail and semi-pro options traders who already seek flow tools, dealer positioning views, and faster execution context.

What drives adoption

Better visual recognition, faster trade framing, confidence in levels, and daily usability. The product needs to feel like a better operating environment, not just another data screen.

What wins this market

Clarity, speed, consistency, and a design system that makes key data points obvious. Traders stay when the tool helps them act faster and with more discipline.

Acquisition metrics

  • Trial-to-paid conversion
  • Landing-page conversion rate
  • Cost to acquire a subscriber
  • Referral / social proof conversion sources

Simple scoreboard

The clean owner scoreboard is: MRR, churn, ARPU, direct cost per user, contribution profit per user, and active daily usage. That set tells you whether the business is getting healthier or weaker.
